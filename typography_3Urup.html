The last word comes from a traditional language in my region called Javanese. Javanese primarily uses the standard alphabet, but it also has a more traditional script known as Aksara Jawa. This script is rarely used today, but you can still find it in certain parts of my city. Yogyakarta, as a city of culture, tradition, and education, is an inseparable part of my identity. I take pride in having been taught this language, and even more so, the Aksara Jawa, back in elementary school. This piece reminds me of home.
The word here, "Urup" (ꦲꦸꦫꦸꦥ꧀), means "to light" or "to shine." This word is commonly used in the daily life of Javanese people. Urup is also part of the well-known expression "urip iku urup," which translates to "life is to shine." This phrase was conveyed by Semar, a character from Javanese wayang (shadow puppetry), as a reminder that in life, we should bring light and benefit to others—just like a candle that burns brightly, illuminating its surroundings.
Just as this word reminds me of my own roots, it carries a deep meaning for me, serving as an essential part of my life motto.
