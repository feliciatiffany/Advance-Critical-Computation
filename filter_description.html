Project Proposal: Hand Gesture Signals for Help
Background
My project comes from the idea of using hand signals and gestures to indicate danger and aims to educate people about their significance. In today's world, where silent communication can be a crucial factor in ensuring personal safety, hand gestures have become vital tools for signaling distress, danger, or the need for rescue.
One notable example is the Signal for Help, created by the Canadian Women’s Foundation in 2020 during the COVID-19 pandemic. This silent hand gesture, designed to help individuals discreetly indicate distress, quickly became viral, demonstrating the urgent need for universally recognized non-verbal signals in society. Inspired by this, the project seeks to integrate hand gesture recognition into an interactive experimental camera using p5.js, with the goal of detecting and visualizing gestures that signify calls for help, raising awareness about non-verbal emergency communication methods.
Chosen Gestures:
  • International Hand Signal for Danger / The Signal for Help (Domestic Violence) – A silent hand gesture indicating distress.
  • ASL for “Help” – An American Sign Language gesture to request assistance.
  • Wave for Rescue (Universal Distress Signal) – A widely recognized motion to signal for help in emergencies.
  • Diver's “Out of Air” Signal – A crucial underwater distress gesture.
This project is motivated by the growing need to raise awareness of these gestures, particularly for individuals in situations where verbal communication is impossible or dangerous. Women, in particular, are disproportionately affected by domestic violence and other forms of abuse, making it essential to emphasize their safety and access to these signals. By creating a camera-based interactive system, users will engage with these gestures in a digital environment, reinforcing their importance and their real-world applications.
Project Implementation
The experimental camera system will be developed using Teachable Machine to train and recognize different hand gestures. Once the model is trained, it will be integrated into p5.js, where gesture recognition will be programmed to trigger visual effects or alerts.
Key Features:
  • DOM Interaction: p5.js will incorporate UI elements that provide real-time feedback based on detected gestures.
  • Graphics and Image Processing: Functions like createGraphics() and loadPixels() will be used to manipulate images and create visual effects.
  • Visual Representation: The camera system will display a black-and-white filtered image, and different hand gestures will trigger distinct illusions or graphics, dynamically responding to the position of the user's hand.
Objectives
By integrating gesture-based emergency signals into a digital camera system, this project aims to:
  • Spread awareness about non-verbal distress signals.
  • Show the importance of silent communication, especially in situations where speaking out isn’t an option.
  • Experiment with interactive camera filters to enhance how gestures are detected and understood.
  • Explore real-world applications in areas like security, mental health, and emergency response.
This project is not only an experiment in camera-based interaction but also a step toward making technology a useful tool for personal safety and social awareness in critical situations.
