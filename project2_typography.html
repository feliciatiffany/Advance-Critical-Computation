The first interactive typography is inspired by the "Oops, no internet" message that typically appears when there is no internet connection on a web browser. I envisioned this as a visual representation of the frustration we feel when encountering such an error. The design features a burst of the word "Oops," symbolizing the mental stress of losing internet access. The scattered dots represent the sudden disarray, as if everything has disappeared in random directions, much like our thoughts in that moment.
